The real problem

You've tried AI. You got no outcome.

You work 13, 14, maybe 16 hours a day. The to-do list never empties. You can't take a day off without something breaking. Everything — every decision, every process, every answer — lives in your head.

You built this business to be free. Instead, the business owns you.

So you tried AI. You connected tools. Set up automations. Maybe hired someone to build workflows. But nothing really changed. The bottlenecks are still there — and now there's more complexity to manage on top of everything else.

"AI doesn't fix a broken process. It scales it."

The problem isn't you. And it isn't the technology. It's that you started automating before you had a system. Before you knew what was worth automating, and why.

  • You automate tasks that shouldn't exist in the first place
  • You build workflows only you understand how to fix
  • You add tools that create new dependencies instead of removing old ones
  • You invest time in setup and see no visible change in how the business runs

What's inside

The AI Business Growth Playbook

A step-by-step guide for business owners who've tried AI automation — and want to finally make it work for their actual business, not a demo version of it.

What the playbook covers — 9 stages
1
Why your business runs on you — not on a system We start with the real problem: everything lives in your head. You'll see exactly why the business stalls the moment you step back — and why adding more tools won't fix it.
2
What a system actually is A simple, clear definition of what a business system looks like in practice. You'll be able to name every system in your business and understand how they connect.
3
Finding your most important system Start with your business goal — then identify which systems are most critical to reaching it. You'll build your business structure inside Notion and know exactly where to focus first.
4
Documenting how your business actually works Map every process inside your key system in enough detail that anyone — with zero background — could run it. No more tribal knowledge. No more "only I know how this works."
5
Deciding what to automate — and what to leave alone A clear framework for evaluating each process: is it repetitive, time-sensitive, or specialist work? You'll categorize every process and know exactly which ones are AI-ready and which aren't.
6
Choosing the right AI tools for each job Not every task needs the same tool. You'll learn what each tool does well — Claude Projects, Claude Skills, Claude Code, Cowork — and match the right one to the right process.
7
Building your first AI agent — step by step We build it together. By the end of this stage, you have a working AI agent running inside your actual business — not a demo, not a template.
8
Evaluating and improving what you've built How to measure whether your AI system is actually working — and how to improve it over time. Includes a practical evaluation checklist you'll use on a regular cadence.
9
The next system — and the loop that keeps going Once the first system runs without you, you do it again. This stage shows you how to repeat the process across every part of your business until you're no longer the bottleneck anywhere.
